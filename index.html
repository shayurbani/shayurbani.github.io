Funny Girls

Could the gender-gap in comedy be narrowing?

By: Shay Urbani

Female stand-up comics in Brooklyn are getting more stage time than in previous years, data from two popular comedy venues shows.

“There’s so much more awareness of the importance of diversity in comedy,” said comedian Calvin Cato, who has been performing and hosting shows in Brooklyn for 11 years. “And there’s interest in supporting different voices.” Cato says that there’s a much wider ranges of voices on stage now than there used to be: more women, more people of color, and more lgbt comics.

In order to test if this was true, I looked at the line-ups from comedy shows in Brooklyn and compared the number of female performers in 2015 and 2017 to see if there was any increase.

“Comedy at The Knitting Factory” in Williamsburg and “The Fancy Show” in Park Slope are both popular, weekly shows that regularly pack a full house and, most importantly, post their featured comics each week on twitter. Like many shows in Brooklyn, these two shows feature comedians "showcase" style: 5-8 comics a show, each short sets. This means that for any given show, we can calculate how much stage time women had as a percentage of the total line up. So, for example, if one night Comedy at the Knitting Factory had 5 comedians performing, and 2 of them were women, we could say that 40% of the line-up that night was female.
